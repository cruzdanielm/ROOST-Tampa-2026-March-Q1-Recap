Executive Summary

A rate-led quarter — March drove the number, the market premium widened.

March delivered $907K of Net Furnished Revenue, 13.5% ahead of budget, at 92.6% occupancy and $325.54 ADR — a $23 rate premium to plan. NOI of $456K came in $72K ahead of budget (+18.8%). Quarter-to-date, the asset delivered $1.14M of NOI on $2.38M of Net Furnished Revenue — $50K (+4.6%) ahead of budget despite January running 13 points below the occupancy plan. The story is clear: Tampa is commanding rate the market cannot match (ARI 128.6 YTD, RGI 129.5 YTD), and February and March made up all of January's softness with room to spare.

The one-line read

Rate leadership is the defining feature of the book — Tampa holds a ~$70 ADR premium over the competitive set and still wins occupancy share, producing RevPAR 34% above the market in March. March occupancy ran 4.7 points ahead of plan, ADR ran $23 ahead, and the two compounded to a 13.5% revenue beat. Q1 YTD landed +3.0% ahead of budget on revenue and +4.6% ahead on NOI after absorbing a soft January (Occ 75.0% vs 88.0% plan). The mix has shifted into short-stay business — transient 1–6 night revenue rose to 68.1% of Q1 from 53.2% last year — driven by Yankees Spring Training and strong event compression. Heading into Q2 the watch items are (1) sustaining the ADR premium as supply normalizes, (2) rebuilding the 30+ night corporate-housing pipeline that stepped down YoY, and (3) protecting the public-review lead (Google 4.7, Tripadvisor 4.8, #8 in Tampa) through peak-season volume.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Rate leadership. Q1 ADR $314 vs $294 PY (+6.9%) and March ADR $325 vs Mar '25 $285 (+13.3%). ARI 128.6 YTD · ARI 128.1 in March (rank #1 of 7 in the comp set every month).
  • March blowout. Occupancy 92.6% at $325.54 ADR produced $907K of Net Furnished Revenue — $108K over budget and the strongest month of the cycle. RevPAR $298.96 vs comp $223.14 (RGI 134.0).
  • Short-stay surge. 1–6 night revenue rose to $1.62M (68.1% of Q1) from $1.23M (53.2%) a year ago — led by NY Yankees Spring Training ($167K, 791 RN), Tampa Bay Lightning ($75K), and event-driven transient demand.
  • NOI resilience. $1.14M Q1 NOI = 47.6% margin on $2.38M Net Furnished Revenue — +4.6% over plan despite January occupancy shortfall absorbed entirely in the back half of the quarter.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Sustain the rate premium. ROOST Tampa commands $70+ of ADR premium over the Downtown comp set. Holding that premium through Q2 as occupancy normalizes post-Spring-Training is the single largest NOI lever.

Rebuild the 30+ night book. Extended-stay (30+) revenue fell to $470K Q1 from $680K PY (-30.9%). The short-stay surge covered it — but a diversified long-stay pipeline (corporate housing, relocation, healthcare travel) is the right Q2 priority to re-anchor shoulder-season performance.

Convert reputation into rate power. Google 4.7 · Tripadvisor 4.8 · Tripadvisor #8 in Tampa. Protecting the ratings lead at peak-season volume and translating it into direct-channel share supports rate discipline when transient demand softens.

Three drivers behind exceeding NOI by $72K vs budget

Revenue & Expense Drivers

What actually moved March & Q1
Demand · Rate · Mix · Cost
Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

Rate on the top line; operating leverage on the P&L.

The Q1 beat is rate-led, not volume-led. March ADR of $325.54 sat $23 above budget and ran $70 above the competitive set — each incremental rate dollar dropped through a cost base that did not have to flex. The margin showed up on the bottom line: March NOI $456K at 50.3% margin (+18.8% YoY) and Q1 NOI $1.14M (+4.6% vs plan) at 47.6% margin.

Revenue drivers

Where the top-line beat originated

  • March rate & occupancy both beat plan. Occupancy 92.65% vs 88% budget; ADR $325.54 vs $302.17. Both sides of the RevPAR equation delivered — RevPAR $298.96 vs $265.91 plan (+12.4%).
  • Yankees Spring Training compression. ~$167K in March off 791 room nights — the single-largest individual account in Q1 and the anchor of March's rate print.
  • Short-stay mix shift pulled rate forward. 1-6 night share of Net Furnished Revenue moved to 68.1% from 53.2% PY — premium-rate segments (sports, transient, compression) taking share from lower-ADR monthly inventory.
  • Downtown Tampa / Water Street compression. 1050 Water Street district demand sustained MPI at 104.6 while ARI reached 128.1 — rate premium on peer-level occupancy.
  • Tampa Bay Lightning & sports layer. Lightning ($75K Q1), Royal Netherlands Air Force training detachment ($27K), and US Army block business ($47K) added a stable group overlay on top of transient demand.
  • ReliaQuest / GRPCORP ecosystem steady. ~$110K Q1 consolidated across ReliaQuest, Communify, and Appspace — institutional base layer beneath the rate-led transient book.

Expense drivers

What held the cost base as volume rose

  • Payroll held flat against rising occupancy. March operating payroll landed on plan despite occupancy outperformance — the departmental staffing model absorbed +4.6 occupancy points without proportional step-up.
  • Utilities tight through peak-demand month. March utilities tracked under variable budget despite full-house nights during Yankees Spring Training — energy-management discipline held through peak.
  • Marketing efficiency. Demand arrived compression-driven — Yankees, Lightning, and Water Street district pull did not require incremental channel spend to convert.
  • Repairs & maintenance flat. Preventive cadence held reactive work down even as occupancy rose; unit-condition spend tracked to plan.
  • Commissions scaling with volume — natural. Third-party and OTA commissions rose with the revenue base — the only major variable line that expanded meaningfully, as expected.
  • Management fees +$ on higher base. Variable escalation on the revenue premium — a favorable signal, not a cost issue.
  • Fixed base (P&I, admin, IT) unchanged. The structurally-fixed layer of the cost base did not flex — every rate dollar above budget landed at near-full contribution margin.
Bottom line

Rate met a structurally-fixed cost base, and the margin showed up. The Q1 outperformance is real operating leverage — the ADR premium over budget and over the competitive set landed at near-full contribution margin because payroll, utilities, marketing, and the fixed base did not have to flex. The counterbalance is symmetrical: the rate premium is currently anchored by Yankees Spring Training and peak-season compression. Sustaining ADR discipline through the April–June transition is what converts the Q1 print into a durable 2026 run-rate.

Heading into Q2 2026
Operational & Commercial
Risks & Opportunities · Q2 Outlook

Four risks to manage and four opportunities to monetize.

Q1 is tracking ahead of plan and the competitive premium has widened. The items that could compress the Q1 flow-through are (a) a rate cliff as Yankees Spring Training rolls off, (b) the 30+ night corporate-housing gap, (c) Tripadvisor ranking headroom, and (d) competitive response to the $70 ADR premium. Peak-season demand, direct-channel conversion, and a corporate-housing rebuild are where the Q2 upside sits.

Risk · Post-Spring-Training Rate Cliff

March ADR $325 was compression-anchored by Yankees Spring Training (~$167K / 791 RN). April MTD ADR is holding at $297 and ARI 131.3, but the structural rate premium needs a replacement demand layer through May–June to avoid give-back.

Risk · 30+ Night Corporate-Housing Gap

30+ night revenue is down ~$114K YoY on LOS mix; share of Net Furnished Revenue dropped from 36.6% to 24.8%. The ballast that carries shoulder periods is thinner — a structural gap to close before off-peak months.

Risk · Tripadvisor Ranking Headroom

Currently #8 of Tampa hotels with a 4.8 rating and 48 reviews. The rating is already top-tier in the comp set — the lever is volume. Moving from #8 into the top 5 requires sustained review velocity through Q2 peak.

Risk · Competitive Rate Response

Tampa ADR +13.3% YoY vs comp +0.8%. The $70 ADR premium (ROOST $300s vs comp set $250s) invites repositioning from Hyatt House, Homewood, and Home2. Defend with product differentiation and direct-channel relationships, not rate.

Opp · Direct-Channel Conversion

Commissions are the main variable line scaling with volume. Moving transient and repeat bookings toward ROOST direct reduces leakage and protects margin as Q2 volume continues to climb.

Opp · Corporate-Housing Rebuild

Target 3–5 net-new 30+ night accounts: healthcare (AdventHealth, Tampa General, Moffitt), tech/corporate (MacDill AFB contractors, Port Tampa Bay), and project-based consulting. Rebuilding this book restores the shoulder-period ballast.

Opp · Tripadvisor Ascent: #8 → Top 5

Review velocity during peak Q2 (Lightning playoffs, summer leisure, sports compression) is the path. Widget placement in confirmation/post-stay flows and front-desk request discipline get the volume the rating already deserves.

Opp · Mid-Stay (7–29 Night) Recovery

This LOS bucket slipped to 15.1% from 22.0%. Project-based consulting, training cohorts, sports season-ticket visitors, and medical travel fit this window. Dedicated pricing and packaging rebuild the middle of the LOS curve.

Strategic summary

Q1 outperformance is rate-led and built on a widened competitive premium. Protecting it requires rate discipline through the post-Spring-Training transition, rebuilding the 30+ night corporate-housing book to carry shoulder periods, and converting the rating advantage into review volume. The upside case: hold ARI above 125 through Q2, add 3–5 long-stay accounts, and move into the Tampa top-5 on Tripadvisor — the 2026 full-year forecast moves materially.
