Executive Summary

A rate-led quarter — March drove the number, the market premium widened.

March delivered $907K of Room Revenue, 13.5% ahead of budget and 19.4% ahead of LY, at 92.6% occupancy and $326 ADR — a $24 rate premium to plan and a $38 premium to March 2025. NOI of $468K came in $84K ahead of budget (+22.0%) and $111K above LY (+31.1%). Quarter-to-date, the asset delivered $1,140K of NOI on $2,250K of Net Revenue (Room $2,384K less commissions) — $57K (+5.3%) ahead of budget and $208K (+22.3%) ahead of Q1 2025, despite January running 13 points below the occupancy plan. The story is clear: Tampa is commanding rate the market cannot match (ARI 128.6 YTD, RGI 129.5 YTD), and February and March made up all of January's softness with room to spare.

The one-line read

Rate leadership is the defining feature of the book — Tampa holds a ~$70 ADR premium over the competitive set and still wins occupancy share, producing RevPAR 34% above the market in March. March occupancy ran 4.7 points ahead of plan, ADR ran $23 ahead, and the two compounded to a 13.5% revenue beat. Q1 YTD landed +3.0% ahead of budget on revenue and +4.6% ahead on NOI after absorbing a soft January (Occ 75.0% vs 88.0% plan). The mix has shifted toward true short-stay business — transient 1–6 night revenue rose to 68.1% of Q1 from 53.2% last year — on organic downtown compression and diversified non-team transient demand. Yankees Spring Training and Tampa Bay Lightning are 30+ night monthly stays that sit in the long-stay bucket. Heading into Q2 the watch items are (1) sustaining the ADR premium as supply normalizes, (2) rebuilding the non-team 30+ night corporate-housing pipeline that stepped down YoY, and (3) protecting the public-review lead (Google 4.7, Tripadvisor 4.8, #6 in Tampa) through peak-season volume.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Rate leadership. Q1 ADR $315 vs $295 PY (+6.9%) and March ADR $326 vs Mar '25 $285 (+13.3%). ARI 128.6 YTD · ARI 128.1 in March (rank #1 of 7 in the comp set every month).
  • March blowout. Occupancy 92.6% at $326 ADR produced $907K of Room Revenue — $108K over budget and the strongest month of the cycle. RevPAR $299 vs comp $223 (RGI 134.0).
  • Short-stay surge. 1–6 night revenue rose to $1,617K (68.1% of Q1) from $1,235K (53.2%) a year ago — driven by organic downtown compression, Water Street corridor demand, and diversified non-team transient volume. (Yankees Spring Training and Lightning sit in the 30+ night monthly-stay bucket, not here.)
  • NOI resilience. $1,140K Q1 NOI = 50.7% margin on $2,250K Net Revenue — +5.3% over plan despite January occupancy shortfall absorbed entirely in the back half of the quarter.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Sustain the rate premium. ROOST Tampa commands $70+ of ADR premium over the Downtown comp set. Holding that premium through Q2 as occupancy normalizes post-Spring-Training is the single largest NOI lever.

Rebuild the non-team 30+ night book. Monthly-stay (30+) revenue fell to $470K Q1 from $680K PY (-31%). Yankees Spring Training (~$167K) and Tampa Bay Lightning (~$75K) sit inside that bucket as 30+ night monthly stays and carry ~51% of it — so the underlying corporate-housing and relocation layer is thinner than the headline implies. A diversified long-stay pipeline (corporate housing, relocation, healthcare travel) is the right Q2 priority to re-anchor shoulder-season performance.

Convert reputation into rate power. Google 4.7 · Tripadvisor 4.8 · Tripadvisor #8 in Tampa. Protecting the ratings lead at peak-season volume and translating it into direct-channel share supports rate discipline when transient demand softens.

What moved the month

Three drivers behind exceeding NOI by $84K vs budget and $111K vs LY

1 · Rate premium held. ADR $326 vs $302 plan ($24 over) and vs $288 LY ($38 over, +13.3% YoY). At 2,785 occupied room nights, every $1 of ADR equals ~$3K; the rate variance alone produced ~$67K of the $108K room revenue beat vs budget and ~$106K of the $147K lift vs LY — true paid-guest ADR, not distorted by comp stays.

2 · Occupancy cushion. 92.65% vs 88.00% plan = ~140 incremental paid room nights vs plan; vs 86.83% LY = ~175 incremental nights YoY. At $326 ADR, the occupancy gains alone generated ~$46K vs budget and ~$57K vs LY on top of the rate beat.

3 · Variable-expense leverage. Operating expenses (ex-commissions) grew 4.4% vs plan and 7.8% vs LY against Net Revenue +13.4% vs plan and +19.4% vs LY — a 9.0-point leverage gap to budget and an 11.6-point gap to LY. Performance-tied lines (management fees +13.5% vs plan, +19.4% vs LY) scaled with revenue, while fixed categories held (utilities +2.2% vs LY).

Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

Rate on the top line; operating leverage on the P&L.

The Q1 beat is rate-led, not volume-led. March ADR of $326 sat $24 above budget and ran $70 above the competitive set — each incremental rate dollar dropped through a cost base that did not have to flex. The margin showed up on the bottom line: March NOI $468K at 54.8% margin on Net Revenue (+22.0% vs plan) and Q1 NOI $1,140K (+5.3% vs plan) at 50.7% margin.

Revenue drivers

Where the top-line beat originated

  • March rate & occupancy both beat plan. Occupancy 92.65% vs 88% budget; ADR $326 vs $302. Both sides of the RevPAR equation delivered — RevPAR $299 vs $266 plan (+12.4%).
  • Yankees Spring Training monthly-stay block anchored the long-stay bucket. ~$167K off 791 room nights as a 30+ night monthly stay — the single-largest individual account in Q1 and the anchor of the 30+ night bucket.
  • True short-stay mix shift pulled rate forward. 1–6 night share of Room Revenue moved to 68.1% from 53.2% PY on organic non-team transient demand — premium-rate downtown compression at a $358 ADR taking share from lower-ADR monthly inventory.
  • Downtown Tampa / Water Street compression. 1050 Water Street district demand sustained MPI at 104.6 while ARI reached 128.1 — rate premium on peer-level occupancy.
  • Tampa Bay Lightning monthly stays + support layer. Lightning ($75K Q1, 290 RN at 30+ nights), Royal Netherlands Air Force training detachment ($27K), and US Army block business ($47K) added a stable long-stay group overlay beneath the transient book.
  • ReliaQuest / GRPCORP ecosystem steady. ~$110K Q1 consolidated across ReliaQuest, Communify, and Appspace — institutional base layer beneath the rate-led transient book.

Expense drivers

What held the cost base as volume rose

  • Payroll held flat against rising occupancy. March operating payroll landed on plan despite occupancy outperformance — the departmental staffing model absorbed +4.6 occupancy points without proportional step-up.
  • Utilities tight through peak-demand month. March utilities tracked under variable budget despite full-house nights during Yankees Spring Training — energy-management discipline held through peak.
  • Marketing efficiency. Demand arrived compression-driven — Yankees, Lightning, and Water Street district pull did not require incremental channel spend to convert.
  • Repairs & maintenance flat. Preventive cadence held reactive work down even as occupancy rose; unit-condition spend tracked to plan.
  • Commissions scaling with volume — natural. Third-party and OTA commissions rose with the revenue base — the only major variable line that expanded meaningfully, as expected.
  • Management fees +$ on higher base. Variable escalation on the revenue premium — a favorable signal, not a cost issue.
  • Fixed base (P&I, admin, IT) unchanged. The structurally-fixed layer of the cost base did not flex — every rate dollar above budget landed at near-full contribution margin.
Bottom line

Rate met a structurally-fixed cost base, and the margin showed up. The Q1 outperformance is real operating leverage — the ADR premium over budget and over the competitive set landed at near-full contribution margin because payroll, utilities, marketing, and the fixed base did not have to flex. The counterbalance is symmetrical: the rate premium is currently anchored by Yankees Spring Training and peak-season compression. Sustaining ADR discipline through the April–June transition is what converts the Q1 print into a durable 2026 run-rate.
